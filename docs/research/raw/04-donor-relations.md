Small–mid Israeli NGOs typically rely on a mix of small recurring individual donors, a handful of local family foundations and corporate CSR partners, plus project‑specific public funding; since October 2023, war‑related giving from Israelis and the diaspora has surged, especially via platforms like JGive, but long‑term donor‑relationship practice in smaller NGOs remains rudimentary and under‑tooled. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/economy/article/rj5vbxbmc)

Below is a focused picture for a Hebrew‑first donor‑CRM product.

## Donor base mix for small NGOs

- Generic fundraising guides for Israeli NGOs describe a typical funding mix: activities and events, membership fees, online campaigns, government grants and support from foundations, reflecting the reality that small NGOs combine community donations with institutional funding rather than relying on a single source. [ech.co](https://ech.co.il/%D7%90%D7%99%D7%9A-%D7%9C%D7%92%D7%99%D7%99%D7%A1-%D7%AA%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%94/)
- Courses like מט״י חיפה’s “כל הדרכים למימון עמותות” explicitly teach NGOs to build a “מפת הכנסות מגוונות ויציבה לעמותה” across philanthropy from Israel and abroad, public funding (מכרזים וקולות קוראים), community crowdfunding, events, and earned income, which implies this multi‑channel mix is considered best practice for small‑mid NGOs. [mati.co](https://mati.co.il/events/ngo-funding/)
- JFN Israel’s Center for Family Philanthropy works with “משפחות פילנתרופיות ישראליות” on intergenerational giving and family‑fund structures, suggesting that family foundations are a meaningful—though relatively concentrated—slice of the donor base for more established NGOs. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/en/family-philanthropy/)
- A 2024 JGive–Ynet analysis shows that in October 2023 alone, about 102 million ₪ were donated through JGive (10× October 2022), with roughly 93,000 donors and 71% of that volume coming from abroad, underscoring how diaspora individuals and foreign‑based organizations became a major donor segment for Israeli NGOs during the war. [jgive](https://www.jgive.com/heb-blog-posts/nytvkh-ntvny-trvmvt-mlkhmt-khrbvt-brzl)

**Post‑October 2023 shift:**

- JGive’s data show that war‑time donations particularly favored NGOs categorized as “מורשת או הנצחה”, “חינוך, השכלה והכשרה מקצועית”, and “ארגוני סנגור, שינוי חברתי ופוליטי”, with advocacy NGOs seeing a 3.5× increase in donations, indicating a thematic shift in donor priorities toward resilience, memory and rights. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/economy/article/rj5vbxbmc)
- Courses launched in 2024–2025 (e.g. מט״י חיפה) now include dedicated sessions on “גיוס משאבים בשעת משבר ומלחמה”, implying that many NGOs had to adjust to crisis‑driven funding patterns and that funders and trainers see this as a structural feature of the donor environment post‑October 2023. [mati.co](https://mati.co.il/events/ngo-funding-3/)

For your product, assume: a long‑tail of small individual donors (often via online platforms), a few “anchor” family foundations and corporate partners, plus project‑based government funding—each with different reporting and relationship needs.

## Donor retention practices at small NGOs

- Practitioner content is explicit that donor retention is often neglected: Amuta Info notes that “תורם שלא מקבל יחס אישי, לא רואה את השפעת תרומתו, או מרגיש שהוא חלק מרשימה אקראית – כנראה לא יתרום שוב”, and contrasts this with the desired state of “תורם שמרגיש מוערך ומעורב בעשייה יהפוך לשותף נאמן לאורך שנים”. [amuta](https://www.amuta.info/%D7%92%D7%99%D7%95%D7%A1-%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D/%D7%9B%D7%9A-%D7%AA%D7%A9%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%95-%D7%A2%D7%9C-%D7%A7%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9B%D7%99-%D7%98%D7%95%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%A2%D7%9D-%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%9D/)
- Zecharia Landau writes that “כל עמותה חייבת להשים דגש על נושא שימור וטיפוח התורמים, ושמירת הקשר עמם במהלך השנה” and discusses why “יותר מ‑50% מהתורמים עוזבים אתכם”, suggesting that significant churn is common and that many NGOs lack systematic retention processes. [zecharialandau](https://zecharialandau.com/newsletter/)
- Both Landau and Amuta Info emphasize basic retention practices—sending immediate, personalized thank‑you messages, regular newsletters, impact stories and small gifts—and frame them as missing in many NGOs: “אם אתם יוצרים קשר עם התורמים שלכם רק כשאתם צריכים תרומה נוספת, אתם מחמיצים הזדמנות”. [amuta](https://www.amuta.info/%D7%92%D7%99%D7%95%D7%A1-%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D/%D7%9B%D7%9A-%D7%AA%D7%A9%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%95-%D7%A2%D7%9C-%D7%A7%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9B%D7%99-%D7%98%D7%95%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%A2%D7%9D-%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%9D/)
- JGive’s own support docs show that NGOs can export detailed donation tables (including donor email, payment method and recurring instructions) but warn “אין להשתמש בכתובת האימייל של התורמים ללא הסכמתם… ניתן לראות האם התורמים הסכימו ליצירת קשר בעמודה הנקראת Donor Share Info”, indicating that the technical ability to track and segment donors exists, but use is constrained by consent and, in practice, often under‑utilized. [intercom](https://intercom.help/jgive-support/he/articles/11931938-%D7%90%D7%99%D7%9A-%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%AA%D7%9F-%D7%9C%D7%A6%D7%A4%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%9B%D7%9C-%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99-%D7%94%D7%AA%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%94)

**Inference for small NGOs (<10 staff):**  
Consultant and trainer content is full of “how to retain donors” basics, which strongly implies that below a certain size, many NGOs do not do consistent segmentation, lifecycle tracking or personalized follow‑ups—they may send occasional mass updates but lack a structured retention program. [course.co](https://www.course.co.il/%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%96_%D7%94%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%9C%D7%99_%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C/%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%A1_%D7%92%D7%99%D7%95%D7%A1_%D7%9B%D7%A1%D7%A4%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA)

## Existing Hebrew content on donor management & fundraising

There is already a substantial Hebrew ecosystem of guides, courses and webinars—mostly aimed at staff in small‑mid NGOs:

- **Articles & guides (free content):**  
  - Amuta Info’s 2025 article “כך תשמרו על קשרים ארוכי טווח עם תורמים – ותגדילו את התרומות לעמותה” covers: immediate, tailored thank‑you letters; monthly/bi‑monthly newsletters; visual storytelling; sharing challenges, not only successes; small gifts; and converting one‑time donors into recurring donors via monthly programs. [amuta](https://www.amuta.info/%D7%92%D7%99%D7%95%D7%A1-%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D/%D7%9B%D7%9A-%D7%AA%D7%A9%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%95-%D7%A2%D7%9C-%D7%A7%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9B%D7%99-%D7%98%D7%95%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%A2%D7%9D-%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%9D/)
  - Ech.co.il’s guide “איך לגייס תרומות לעמותה? אתם שואלים – אנחנו עונים!” walks through fundraising events, membership fees via הוראת קבע, online fundraising, and government/foundation grants, positioning fundraising as a multi‑channel operational task tied to proper economic management. [ech.co](https://ech.co.il/%D7%90%D7%99%D7%9A-%D7%9C%D7%92%D7%99%D7%99%D7%A1-%D7%AA%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%94/)
  - Landau’s content on donor retention and his “קורס אסטרטגיה לגיוס משאבים” focus on building an annual fundraising plan, measuring performance, and treating newsletters as the core, low‑cost retention touchpoint. [zecharialandau](https://zecharialandau.com/strategy-course-info/)

- **Courses & training programs:**  
  - המי״ל (המרכז הישראלי לניהול) runs a “קורס גיוס כספים ומשאבים לעמותות” that teaches “גיוס כספים מקצועי ומודרני”, work with foundations, strategic and annual planning, marketing for fundraising, and “ההתרמה האישית והישירה”, clearly aimed at making NGOs more professional in donor relations. [course.co](https://www.course.co.il/%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%96_%D7%94%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%9C%D7%99_%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C/%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%A1_%D7%92%D7%99%D7%95%D7%A1_%D7%9B%D7%A1%D7%A4%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%90%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA)
  - מט״י חיפה’s courses “גיוס משאבים : מחזור #2 לקורס מימון עמותות” and “כל הדרכים למימון עמותות” cover: philanthropy (Israel & abroad), public funding, earned income, crowdfunding and events, legal aspects of NGO funding, and “שימוש ב‑AI לגיוס משאבים מהיר יותר”, explicitly targeting NGO managers and board members. [mati.co](https://mati.co.il/events/ngo-funding/)
  - Bar‑Ilan’s “גיוס ופיתוח משאבים – אסטרטגיה ופרקטיקה” trains fundraisers to “להשתמש בבינה מלאכותית, לחזק קשרי תורמים, למקסם הזדמנויות מימון”, indicating that AI‑enhanced donor management is becoming part of professional curricula. [akadima.biu.ac](https://akadima.biu.ac.il/course-2196)
  - Atlas, Mesimot and other private consultancies offer courses and mentoring; Atlas describes training “גייסי משאבים” with broad skills in grant writing, donor relations and funding strategy, and also sells a subscription platform with a “סוכן חכם” that pushes funding opportunities, stepping into tech‑enabled fundraising. [atlas-grants](https://atlas-grants.com/he/course/)

**What’s missing (opportunity for your content/product):**

- Most content is **didactic**, not tool‑driven: lots of “what you should do” around thank‑you letters, newsletters and segmentation, but very little that shows NGOs how to operationalize this inside a simple, Hebrew‑first CRM workflow. [zecharialandau](https://zecharialandau.com/newsletter/)
- Many trainings are targeted at **professional fundraisers** (courses costing thousands of ₪ and assuming a dedicated role), leaving a gap for micro‑NGOs where the director or coordinator does fundraising part‑time and needs lightweight, opinionated tooling rather than abstract strategy. [akadima.biu.ac](https://akadima.biu.ac.il/course-2196)

## AI in Israeli fundraising and donor management

- A 2026 guide “AI לעמותות ולמלכ\"רים בישראל 2026: גיוס תרומות, ניהול מתנדבים ובחירת שותף הטמעה” describes AI as a way to unify donation, volunteer and regulatory‑reporting data in one system, including use cases like prioritizing donor outreach, automating thank‑you messages and drafting grant proposals. [stsiconic](https://stsiconic.com/ai-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9C%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%92%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%9C%D7%9C%D7%90-%D7%9E%D7%98%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%91%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8/)
- Tzedek.me’s article “בינה מלאכותית (AI) לעמותות: 21 משימות שחוסכות עשרות שעות בחודש” lists practical tasks: grant writing, donor thank‑you letters, funder reports, segmentation of donor lists, and drafting newsletters—explicitly “לא תיאוריה” but concrete tasks NGOs can automate. [tzedek](https://tzedek.me/ai-nonprofits)
- Training programs for fundraising now include AI modules: מט״י חיפה’s courses teach “שימוש ב‑AI לגיוס משאבים מהיר יותר”, and Bar‑Ilan’s course highlights using AI to strengthen donor relationships and design smarter campaigns. [mati.co](https://mati.co.il/events/ngo-funding-3/)
- Atlas’s subscription platform promotes a “סוכן חכם” that pushes relevant funding calls and grant opportunities to NGOs, functioning as an AI‑like recommendation layer over funding data. [atlas-grants](https://atlas-grants.com/he/)

At this point, AI in Israeli NGO fundraising is mostly discussed as:

- A **writing & automation assistant** for grants, donor emails, reports and newsletters. [stsiconic](https://stsiconic.com/ai-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9C%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%92%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%9C%D7%9C%D7%90-%D7%9E%D7%98%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%91%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8/)
- A **smart alert/insight layer** (e.g., Atlas’s “סוכן חכם”) over funding opportunities, not yet mainstream predictive donor scoring for small NGOs. [atlas-grants](https://atlas-grants.com/he/)
- A topic in **courses and consulting**, rather than integrated into everyday workflows of small NGOs—which is precisely where a Hebrew‑first CRM with embedded, constrained‑cost AI could differentiate.

***

For a donor‑CRM product, the evidence points to three clear design levers:

1. Make it trivial for a 3–10‑person NGO to move from “Excel + JGive exports + ad‑hoc newsletters” to **structured donor lifecycle tracking and segmentation**. [intercom](https://intercom.help/jgive-support/he/articles/11931938-%D7%90%D7%99%D7%9A-%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%AA%D7%9F-%D7%9C%D7%A6%D7%A4%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%9B%D7%9C-%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99-%D7%94%D7%AA%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%94)
2. Bake in **retention patterns** (thank‑you flows, newsletters, impact storytelling) as defaults, not optional extras, using the phrasing and logic from the existing Hebrew guides. [zecharialandau](https://zecharialandau.com/newsletter/)
3. Offer **AI that does very specific, high‑leverage tasks**—grant drafts, personalized thank‑you copy, donor‑segment summaries—inside a Hebrew UI, with clear limits and predictable cost, instead of abstract “AI agents”. [tzedek](https://tzedek.me/ai-nonprofits)
