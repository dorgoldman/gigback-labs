Israeli NGOs use a mix of local Hebrew‑first CRM/donation platforms, generic CRMs, and a lot of “Excel/WhatsApp‑driven” workflows, with Monday.com and Google Workspace increasingly providing the project/governance layer; the biggest gaps are integrated, Hebrew‑native workflows and tools that small NGOs can actually implement without consultants. [israeltoremet](https://www.israeltoremet.org/nonprofit/crm)

Below is a tool‑by‑tool map, with emphasis on small NGOs (<10 staff) and community complaints.

## Donor‑management & CRM tools actually in use

### Local Hebrew‑first / Israel‑focused CRMs

- **IsraelGives / ישראל תורמת (IsraelGives CRM + Payments+)**  
  IsraelGives positions itself as “פלטפורמת התרומות המובילה בישראל מאז 2009” and offers a free nonprofit CRM that manages donors, leads and mailing lists, integrated with its fundraising pages and payment processing. [israelgives](https://www.israelgives.org/igindex.aspx?lang=he)
  Their Payments+ service is marketed specifically to NGOs with very low card‑processing fees (starting at 0.36% and standard 1%) and donor‑facing upsell to cover fees, with forms available in Hebrew, English and 7 other languages. [israeltoremet](https://www.israeltoremet.org/nonprofit/payments)

- **JGive (עמותת JGive)**  
  JGive describes its offering as “כל מה שעמותה צריכה במקום אחד: ניהול תורמים מתקדם, דפי סליקה, קמפיינים לגיוס המונים ו‑CRM”, explicitly framing the platform as a donor CRM plus online campaigns with subsidized fees for NGOs. [jgive](https://www.jgive.com/solutions/charities/raise)
  War‑time reports show thousands of NGOs using JGive for campaign and recurring donations, suggesting that JGive acts as both fundraising infrastructure and a de‑facto donor database for many small and mid‑size organizations. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/economy/article/rj5vbxbmc)

- **Powerlink / Fireberry (CRM לעמותות)**  
  Powerlink markets a “מערכת CRM ללא עלות לעמותות וארגונים ללא מטרות רווח” for donation and donor management, promising free access for NGOs as part of its social mission. [pages.powerlink.co](https://pages.powerlink.co.il/nonprofit)
  Fireberry (rebranded from Powerlink) has a dedicated vertical page for NGOs, pitching itself as a CRM to manage donors, volunteers and sponsors and “לשחרר זמן יקר” by automating repetitive tasks. [fireberry](https://www.fireberry.com/he/industries/non-profit)

- **APT, נדרים פלוס, קשר/קהילות, Kala CRM, ToAssociate, others**  
  A consultant’s 2025 review of NGO CRMs lists APT (אי.פי.טי), Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud and נדרים פלוס as three leading donor‑management systems, with APT described as “מותאמת במיוחד לשוק הישראלי, ממשק בעברית, התאמה מלאה לדרישות החוק, תמיכה טובה בהוראות קבע בנקאיות”, and נדרים פלוס positioned as low‑cost and simple for small–mid NGOs. [tfr.co](https://www.tfr.co.il/%D7%A1%D7%A7%D7%99%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%A9%D7%9C-3-%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%99-%D7%98%D7%95%D7%91%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C-%D7%99%D7%A2%D7%99%D7%9C/)
  The same review mentions קשר/קהילות as a simple, donation‑focused tool with “יכולות CRM בסיסיות בלבד”, suitable for small communities and NGOs, while an NGO‑oriented Facebook discussion recommends Kala CRM as “הכי מתאימה מראש שאני מכיר” for NGOs that want to manage donors, activities and volunteers in one system. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/DigitalBizGroup/posts/27835599556088175/)

- **Frontask, Unitech, QuintaDB, Tazman, ToAssociate**  
  Frontask CRM offers a Hebrew‑language NGO CRM with built‑in donation forms and client‑side charging for “כפתורי תרומה ישירים” across websites, landing pages and email. [frontask.co](https://www.frontask.co.il/crm-for-nonprofit-organization/)
  Unitech markets a “מערכת CRM לעמותות” for donors, volunteers and beneficiaries, and QuintaDB promotes a donor‑management database builder that can be embedded in NGO sites. [uni-tech.co](https://uni-tech.co.il/crm-for-ngos/)
  Tazman’s 2026 guide to “תוכנה לניהול עמותות ומלכ\"רים” explicitly frames a dedicated NGO system as a combined CRM for donors and for operational entities (members, volunteers, beneficiaries) in one platform, with features for סעיף 46 receipts and compliance; ToAssociate similarly sells an all‑in‑one NGO system for donations, receipts and accounting. [toassociate](https://www.toassociate.com/)

### Global CRMs and generic tools

- **Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud & other enterprise CRMs**  
  Israeli NGO tech advisors repeatedly highlight Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud as a powerful but complex choice: “מערכת גמישה ועוצמתית מאוד… אבל עלות הטמעה והתאמה גבוהה, דורשת מומחיות ייעודית, פחות מותאמת לשוק הישראלי”, recommending it mostly for large NGOs with international operations. [amuta](https://www.amuta.info/%D7%99%D7%A2%D7%95%D7%A5-%D7%90%D7%A8%D7%92%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99/%D7%90%D7%99%D7%9A-%D7%91%D7%95%D7%97%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%AA-crm-%D7%95%D7%90%D7%99%D7%96%D7%95-%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%9C%D7%A6%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95/)
  Other global CRMs (Zoho, Microsoft Dynamics, Pipedrive, Origami, etc.) surface in Israeli content, but they are usually suggested by consultants rather than organically adopted by small NGOs. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oVUv3MqIRyQ)

- **Informal tools (Excel, Google Sheets, Gmail, WhatsApp)**  
  Guides aimed at NGOs and social‑sector organizations note that many small entities manage donors and volunteers with spreadsheets and basic cloud tools, often described as “הכל מפוזר בין האקלסים” and lacking a single source of truth. [israelcrm.co](https://israelcrm.co.il/%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%AA-crm-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%94-%D7%97%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%91-%D7%9C%D7%93%D7%A2%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9C%D7%9E%D7%94-%D7%A7%D7%A9%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%9E/)
  Google’s “מידע על Google לעמותות” and its volunteer‑management guides explicitly encourage NGOs to use Google Workspace, Docs, Sheets, Forms and Calendar to recruit and manage volunteers, showing that these tools are part of mainstream NGO workflows. [support.google](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/answer/1614581?hl=he-IL)
  A 2025 article on WhatsApp as a work tool for NGOs argues that “וואטסאפ יכול להפוך לכלי רב‑עוצמה בניהול קשרי תורמים, גיוס מתנדבים, תמיכה באוכלוסיות… קבוצות וואטסאפ הן כלי מושלם לשימור מעורבות בקהילות”, confirming that many NGOs coordinate donors and volunteers via WhatsApp groups rather than formal CRMs. [peach.co](https://peach.co.il/%D7%95%D7%95%D7%90%D7%98%D7%A1%D7%90%D7%A4-%D7%96%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%90-%D7%A8%D7%A7-%D7%A6%D7%90%D7%98-%D7%96%D7%94-%D7%9B%D7%9C%D7%99-%D7%A2%D7%91%D7%95%D7%93%D7%94/)

### What do small NGOs (<10 staff) most commonly rely on?

- NGO‑focused reviews and community posts consistently recommend low‑cost, Israel‑specific CRMs such as נדרים פלוס, קשר/קהילות, Kala CRM, IsraelGives/JGive and Powerlink/Fireberry for small NGOs that cannot afford heavy implementation projects. [pages.powerlink.co](https://pages.powerlink.co.il/nonprofit)
- At the same time, multiple practitioners note that many micro‑NGOs stay on Excel/Google Sheets + WhatsApp/Gmail, only adding a donation platform (IsraelGives, JGive, נדרים פלוס) for payment processing and basic donor history, rather than a full‑featured CRM. [israeltoremet](https://www.israeltoremet.org/nonprofit/payments)

For your product strategy, this implies a very large base of NGOs with ad‑hoc donor lists in spreadsheets and messaging apps, and a smaller but growing segment using Israel‑centric CRMs whose UX and integrations are often limited.

## Project/task management & Monday.com in NGO workflows

### Tools in use

- **Monday.com (Work Management + Monday CRM)**  
  Monday.com is widely promoted to Israeli NGOs as a “מוח ארגוני” that centralizes project, process and donor/volunteer management; partners describe use cases such as tracking food distribution projects, volunteer shifts and donor relations on boards. [crmers.co](https://crmers.co.il/monday-for-nonprofits/)
  NGO‑oriented implementation firms (Minuf, BringUp, Web3D, Shatil/Shift) actively market Monday for nonprofits, provide Hebrew‑language training, and highlight ready‑made templates for donors, grants, volunteers and program management. [minuf.co](https://minuf.co.il/monday-for-non-profits/)

- **ClickUp and other generic PM tools**  
  Israeli productivity bloggers and consultants recommend ClickUp as an all‑in‑one project management tool and compare it with Monday and Asana; while these reviews target businesses, NGOs appear in the examples and the tools are frequently suggested to “עמותות” seeking modern task management. [dvirnaaman.co](https://dvirnaaman.co.il/tools-systems/clickup-tool-review/)
  Blogs on Hebrew task‑management software list several tools with Hebrew interfaces (often business‑oriented) that NGOs can repurpose: ClickUp, Monday, Trello, Notion and others, but they are not NGO‑specific. [badakti.co](https://badakti.co.il/task-management-in-hebrew/)

- **Google Workspace and simple tools**  
  Google’s nonprofit program gives eligible NGOs free or discounted Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Docs, Sheets, Meet), and Google publishes a Hebrew guide on using these tools to coordinate volunteers—essentially turning Workspace into a basic project/task system for many organizations. [support.google](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/answer/9333814?hl=iw)

### Monday.com pricing and discounts for Israeli NGOs

- Monday’s official nonprofit program offers “10 first seats for free, and a 70% discount per seat from the 11th seat” on its Nonprofit Plan, plus a 33% discount on Enterprise plans, with eligibility based on recognized charity/nonprofit status and a one‑year subscription commitment. [monday](https://monday.com/nonprofits/eligibility)
- Israeli implementation partners echo this, writing that “אם אתם עמותה קטנה / בינונית תוכלו להשתמש במאנדיי ללא עלות כספית כלל… במידה ותצטרכו רישיונות נוספים, מאנדיי מעניקה אותם לעמותות ב‑70% הנחה”, and some mention even 80% discounts in specific arrangements. [minuf.co](https://minuf.co.il/tikun13-thank/)

- Globally in 2026, Monday’s core Work Management pricing (non‑discounted) is around 9–12 USD per seat per month on annual billing for Basic/Standard, with a 3‑seat minimum; reviews note that for typical teams effective costs often run higher once add‑ons and Pro features are included. [monday](https://monday.com/pricing)
- For a small Israeli NGO under 10 staff that qualifies for the nonprofit program, this practically means project and CRM boards can be run for free or at heavily subsidized prices, with costs beyond the tenth seat depending on how many extra seats and whether AI credits are purchased. [mako.co](https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-c2592b06ab7a491026.htm)

### Language and RTL support; common complaints

- A 2024 article titled “מערכת מאנדיי בעברית – יש חיה כזאת” notes that despite being Israeli‑built, Monday is still oriented to its global market: the UI is mainly English, and “אין עדיין מערכת מאנדיי בעברית מלאה”, with the interface remaining left‑to‑right even when Hebrew text is used. [crmers.co](https://crmers.co.il/monday-system-in-hebrew/)
- The same piece explains that while many labels are translated and some adaptations exist for Israelis, a fully Hebrew/RTL UI (“ממשק… מימין לשמאל”) does not yet exist, which is a recurring complaint among Hebrew‑speaking users in NGO training sessions. [shiftshatil.org](https://shiftshatil.org.il/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%A0%D7%93%D7%99%D7%99-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA/)

- A 2025 study “מחקר על החסמים בהטמעת מערכת Monday.com בארגונים חברתיים בישראל” analyzes technical, organizational and cultural barriers, including board‑level skepticism, lack of internal tech capacity, and difficulty adapting Monday to very small NGOs, especially advocacy organizations vs. service‑delivery NGOs. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/barriers-monday-implementation-social-orgs-israel)
- A 2025–2026 guide on Monday AI credits for nonprofits warns that NGOs get 500 AI credits per month free, but “רכישת 500 קרדיטים נוספים עולה 200$ בחודש (אין כרגע מסלול הנחה לעמותות)”, and gives real examples of an NGO accidentally burning all credits on one bulk operation—leading to frustration about hidden AI costs. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/monday-ai-credits-for-nonprofits)

Common user sentiments from Hebrew‑language blogs and NGO trainings include:
- Monday is powerful but “כבדה” and overkill for tiny NGOs without IT staff.  
- Confusion over product split (Work Management vs. Monday CRM) and how to structure NGO workflows.  
- Annoyance about the LTR interface when working heavily in Hebrew and the complexity of permissions/automations.  

These pain points are important if you consider positioning a simpler, Hebrew‑native, NGO‑specific PM/CRM tool.

## AI tools and “AI agents” in Israeli NGOs (2025–2026)

- A 2026 Hebrew article “בינה מלאכותית (AI) לעמותות: 21 משימות שחוסכות עשרות שעות בחודש” describes concrete NGO use cases: AI for grant writing, drafting donor thank‑you letters, progress reports, volunteer recruitment messages, and summarizing board documents and surveys—explicitly targeting NGOs that are starting to adopt tools such as ChatGPT and other LLMs. [tzedek](https://tzedek.me/ai-nonprofits)
- The author emphasizes “לא תיאוריה… מאיפה מתחילים: כתיבת בקשות מענק + מכתבי תודה ודיווח לתורמים”, suggesting that grant applications and donor communications are the first adoption beachheads. [tzedek](https://tzedek.me/ai-nonprofits)

- Monday AI is highlighted in NGO‑specific content as a way to automatically summarize CVs, analyze feedback forms and improve writing in WorkDocs and board updates, “גם בעברית”, with cautionary guidance on managing the monthly AI‑credit budget. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/monday-ai-nonprofits-credit-usage-guide)
- Implementation partners note that “יותר ויותר עמותות עושות שימוש בכלי ה‑AI המובנים בתוך Monday”, indicating that Monday AI is one of the first structured AI tools many NGOs encounter, embedded in their existing PM stack. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/monday-ai-nonprofits-credit-usage-guide)

- Google’s nonprofit program doesn’t frame its tools as “AI” per se, but Workspace AI features (smart compose, document suggestions) and Ads Grants (for outreach) are part of the broader digital‑tool ecosystem NGOs tap into, particularly for communications and outreach. [support.google](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/answer/1614581?hl=he-IL)

Overall, AI adoption among Israeli NGOs appears pragmatic and task‑focused: starting with text‑generation (grants, donor emails), summarization (reports, feedback), and some automation inside Monday; standalone “AI agents” for workflow orchestration are not yet prominent in the public NGO discourse, which is an opportunity area. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/monday-ai-credits-for-nonprofits)

## Complaints and pain points from community discussions

From Hebrew‑language forums, Facebook groups and NGO tech blogs, several recurring themes emerge:

- **Fragmentation and multi‑tool overload**  
  A Facebook post asking “מישהו מכיר מערכת CRM לניהול תרומות?” describes the reality as “ניהול תורמים. פעילויות. מתנדבים למה לפצל כל דבר למערכת נפרדת?”, reflecting frustration that NGOs must juggle multiple systems for donors, volunteers and programs instead of one integrated tool. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/DigitalBizGroup/posts/27835599556088175/)
  ISRAEL CRM’s article on NGO CRMs notes that “כיום פתרון לעמותות נמצא בפעילות משולבת בין מערכות CRM לאינטגרציות ואוטומציות מחוץ למערכות אלו”, i.e., NGOs often need a combination of CRM plus external integrations and automations because no single system covers all their needs. [israelcrm.co](https://israelcrm.co.il/%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%AA-crm-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%94-%D7%97%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%91-%D7%9C%D7%93%D7%A2%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9C%D7%9E%D7%94-%D7%A7%D7%A9%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%9E/)

- **Cost and implementation complexity**  
  Reviews of APT and Salesforce stress that while these systems are powerful, “עלות גבוהה יחסית” and the need for expert implementers make them suitable mainly for mid‑to‑large NGOs; small NGOs risk overpaying or under‑utilizing advanced systems. [tfr.co](https://www.tfr.co.il/%D7%A1%D7%A7%D7%99%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%A9%D7%9C-3-%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%99-%D7%98%D7%95%D7%91%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C-%D7%99%D7%A2%D7%99%D7%9C/)
  Monday implementation research points to budget constraints, lack of internal champions, and cultural resistance (e.g., boards or senior staff skeptical of spending time and money on system configuration) as major barriers in social organizations. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/barriers-monday-implementation-social-orgs-israel)

- **Language/UI issues (Hebrew & RTL)**  
  Users and consultants complain that Monday and many global tools are not fully RTL: articles explicitly state that a “ממשק… מימין לשמאל… כפי שזה עדיין מתקיים כעת” is missing, and that despite some Hebrew labels, the LTR layout can be cognitively taxing for staff who work mostly in Hebrew. [crmers.co](https://crmers.co.il/monday-system-in-hebrew/)
  This is echoed in NGO trainings where staff report difficulty reading mixed Hebrew/English boards and emails, and a desire for fully Hebrew interfaces, not just translated menus. [shiftshatil.org](https://shiftshatil.org.il/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%A0%D7%93%D7%99%D7%99-%D7%9C%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA/)

- **Hidden or non‑obvious costs (especially AI and seats)**  
  Monday AI’s credit model leads to scenarios where one bulk action consumes all monthly AI credits, forcing NGOs to either wait or pay 200 USD for 500 extra credits—without a nonprofit discount—something practitioners call out as a trap for resource‑constrained NGOs. [ehudst.co](https://www.ehudst.co.il/post/monday-ai-credits-for-nonprofits)
  Global pricing reviews emphasize that Monday’s seat minimums and price increases can surprise small teams; while NGO discounts mitigate this, many organizations struggle to understand the real long‑term cost of combining Work Management, CRM and AI. [frontdeskreview](https://frontdeskreview.com/software/project-management/monday-com/)

- **Staying stuck in spreadsheets and WhatsApp**  
  Several NGO‑focused guides and consultants note that organizations “מפספסות הזדמנויות רבות” by staying in Excel and disorganized tools, but also acknowledge that no‑code and structured PM/CRM tools feel intimidating; NGOs fear migrating data and losing control even when current tools are clearly suboptimal. [tazman.co](https://tazman.co.il/blog/%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%9C%D7%9B%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D)

These complaints directly inform where a new product can reduce friction: one integrated, Hebrew‑native workflow; transparent, NGO‑friendly pricing; simple migration from Excel/Sheets; and minimal configuration overhead for non‑technical staff.

## Hebrew‑first NGO tech products and adjacent competitors

For CRM, task management and governance/compliance, the main Hebrew‑first or Israel‑focused products you should consider as direct or adjacent competitors are:

- **IsraelGives / ישראל תורמת** – fundraising platform + free nonprofit CRM + Payments+ module for low‑fee card processing, multilingual donation forms and donor‑history integration. [israeltoremet](https://www.israeltoremet.org/crm)
- **JGive** – NGO‑run fundraising infrastructure with built‑in CRM, campaign tools and subsidized fees, widely used in war‑time donation surges. [jgive](https://www.jgive.com/solutions/charities/raise)
- **Powerlink / Fireberry** – Israeli CRM with a dedicated NGO vertical and free licenses for NGOs, used for donors, volunteers and sponsors. [fireberry](https://www.fireberry.com/he/industries/non-profit)
- **APT, נדרים פלוס, קשר/קהילות, Kala CRM** – Israel‑specific donor management systems with Hebrew UI and bank debit support, focused on recurring donations and simple NGO campaigns. [aptsoftware.co](https://aptsoftware.co.il/)
- **Frontask, Unitech, Tazman, ToAssociate** – NGO‑oriented systems for donations, receipts, members, volunteers and financial compliance (including סעיף 46 and “חשבוניות ישראל” requirements). [frontask.co](https://www.frontask.co.il/crm-for-nonprofit-organization/)
- **Monday.com (Work Management + Monday CRM for Nonprofits)** – not Hebrew‑native, but deeply entrenched as the go‑to PM and process platform for many mid‑size NGOs, with generous seat discounts and growing AI features; multiple Israeli firms specialize in its implementation for NGOs. [minuf.co](https://minuf.co.il/monday-for-nonprofits/)
- **Generic PM/CRM tools repurposed for NGOs** – ClickUp, Trello, Google Workspace, and global CRMs (Salesforce, Zoho, etc.) used via local consultants; they are not NGO‑specific but do occupy the “digital stack” mindshare among advanced organizations. [badakti.co](https://badakti.co.il/task-management-in-hebrew/)

From a differentiation standpoint, the most open space appears to be:
- A **Hebrew‑first, RTL‑native**, NGO‑specific CRM + task/board system that is genuinely usable by teams of 3–10 without consultant help.  
- Deep integration with Israel‑specific needs (ניהול תקין, סעיף 46 receipts, bank הוראת קבע, reporting to funders) and donor/volunteer storytelling.  
- Opinionated templates for grants, donor journeys and project tracking, plus light AI assistance that is predictable in cost and tuned to NGO language and workflows. [tazman.co](https://tazman.co.il/blog/%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%94%D7%95%D7%9C-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%9C%D7%9B%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D)
