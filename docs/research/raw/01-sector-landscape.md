Israel’s third sector is large, fragmented, and dominated by small NGOs, with tens of thousands of registered עמותות but only about half actively operating, and strong recent shifts in funding toward war‑related, welfare and resilience causes. [agentskills.co](https://agentskills.co.il/he/mcp/israel-amutot)

Below is a sourced snapshot structured for product/content strategy, with a focus on what is actually measurable today and where the data gaps are.

## Number of registered NGOs and active vs. dormant

- A 2020 summary from JDC‑ELKA and Guidestar noted that as of June 2020 “יותר מ‑37,000 עמותות וכמעט 1,500 חל״צ” were registered with רשם העמותות and רשם ההקדשות. [thejoint.org](https://www.thejoint.org.il/news/17/)
- The same piece states that only about 20,000 of these עמותות וחל״צ were “פעילות באחת משלוש השנים האחרונות”, i.e., had filed activity in at least one of the three preceding years, implying roughly half of registered entities were dormant by the authority’s operational definition in 2020. [thejoint.org](https://www.thejoint.org.il/news/17/)

- Guidestar’s 2018 yearbook reports that in 2018 there were 19,205 active עמותות and 743 חל״צ (public‑benefit companies) operating in Israel. [ctg.org](https://ctg.org.il/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018/)
- This 2018 figure is explicitly “פעלו בשנת 2018”, i.e., organizations with recorded activity, not just legally registered entities, and is therefore a better proxy for the actively operating universe at that time. [ctg.org](https://ctg.org.il/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018/)

- A 2026 MCP‑based mirror of the Ministry of Justice dataset (“מאגר עמותות וחברות לתועלת הציבור”) states that its search index covers 74,880 registered עמותות plus around 1,500 חל״צ, all sourced from רשות התאגידים via data.gov.il. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)
- These 74,880 records include historical and dissolved entities, so they represent the cumulative registry rather than currently operating NGOs; no official post‑2020 count of “active in last 3 years” has yet been published in the sources found. [agentskills.co](https://agentskills.co.il/he/mcp/israel-amutot)

**Implication for your strategy:** you can safely assume a registered universe approaching 75k עמותות, but an actively operating core closer to ~20–25k, based on 2018–2020 operational counts and the ongoing growth in registrations. [agentskills.co](https://agentskills.co.il/he/mcp/israel-amutot)

## Size and budget distribution (small vs. mid vs. large)

- Guidestar’s 2018 yearbook (מכון למשפט ופילנתרופיה, אונ’ ת״א, עבור גיידסטאר, רשות התאגידים והג׳וינט) is the only systematic, national‑level profiling of NGO size; it classifies organizations by revenues and fields of activity and was designed to be “מקיף ופורץ דרך”. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)
- The yearbook focuses extensively on the prevalence of small, volunteer‑based organizations and shows that most Israeli NGOs have relatively low annual budgets compared to a small group of very large welfare, education, health and religious bodies, although exact percentage distributions are not exposed in the public summary we have. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)

- The same ecosystem description (Guidestar/JDC) emphasizes that many NGOs depend on a handful of paid staff plus volunteers, especially in welfare, local community and niche advocacy organizations, again indicating a long‑tail of micro‑NGOs and a thin stratum of professionalized, mid‑ to large‑scale bodies. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)
- The JDC‑ELKA data portal (“מאגר מידע – עמותות וחברות לתועלת הציבור”) explicitly provides raw tables that include financial fields and some HR‑related fields (e.g. employees, volunteers) for each NGO, updated weekly, intended for analytical work by researchers and policy actors. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)

**Data gap:** none of the post‑2018 public summaries found give a clear, updated breakdown such as “X% of NGOs have 1–5 staff, Y% 6–50, Z% >50” or a precise median budget figure; these can be computed, but only by analyzing the JDC‑ELKA / data.gov.il tables directly. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)

**Practical takeaway:** for product/content targeting, assume:
- A very large base of low‑budget, low‑staff NGOs (often 0–5 employees plus volunteers).  
- A smaller layer of mid‑sized service providers (tens of staff, multi‑million NIS budgets).  
- A small top tier of large organizations (major health, welfare, education, religious and national NGOs) that dominate total spending but are fewer in number. [ctg.org](https://ctg.org.il/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018/)

## Total sector revenue and breakdown by cause area

- Guidestar’s 2018 yearbook reports “סך ההכנסות של כלל העמותות” for that year and presents detailed breakdowns by field (חינוך, רווחה, בריאות, דת, סביבה, תרבות וכו׳), positioning the yearbook as a baseline for understanding the economic footprint of the third sector. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)
- The public summary confirms that welfare, education and religion‑related NGOs constitute the largest share of organizations and revenues, with environment and culture representing smaller but important niches. [ctg.org](https://ctg.org.il/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018/)

- The JDC‑ELKA portal’s four datasets (עמותות, חל״צ, foreign government donations, and ניהול תקין) include, for each organization, financial variables that allow reconstruction of total revenues and expenditures by NACE‑like activity codes used by רשם העמותות. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)
- These datasets are updated weekly and are explicitly intended for tasks such as “ניתוח מגזרי” and “תכנון פוליסות ציבוריות”, but they are raw CSVs rather than pre‑aggregated dashboards, so any up‑to‑date totals and cause‑area breakdowns require your own data work. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)

**Important limitation:** the sources accessed do not provide a recent (post‑2018) published figure like “total NGO revenues were X NIS in 2023” or a ready‑made 2024–2026 cause‑area pie chart; you can, however, reconstruct such numbers from the JDC‑ELKA datasets for your specific product strategy. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)

## Structural trends 2024–2026

### War‑related donation shifts (חרבות ברזל)

- JGive’s data, as reported by Ynet in May 2024, shows that in October 2023 alone roughly 102 million NIS were donated via the platform—ten times the amount donated in October 2022—and the number of donors tripled to about 93,000. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/economy/article/rj5vbxbmc)
- The same report notes that the share of donations from abroad via JGive jumped to 71% in October 2023, compared with 21% in the months before the war, highlighting the central role of diaspora giving during the crisis. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/economy/article/rj5vbxbmc)

- A 2026 Bizportal analysis of US‑based “friends of Israel” organizations finds that donations to Israel reached about 4.1 billion USD during the war period, a 14% increase, with around 100 organizations responsible for 80% of the funds and expectations that 2024–2025 volumes will be even higher. [bizportal.co](https://www.bizportal.co.il/general/news/article/20030462)
- Jewish People Policy Institute’s 2024 annual assessment cites a digital donation platform that aggregates around 8,300 NGOs and has raised more than 500 million NIS cumulatively, linking the surge in philanthropic activity to October 7 and its aftermath. [jppi.org](https://jppi.org.il/he/%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%94-%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%AA-2024/s/6.5/)

- A survey summarized by Walla! in April 2024 shows donors shifting priorities: post‑war, top destinations became charities for the needy, security forces and evacuees, while donations to cancer and disability NGOs dropped to fifth and sixth place. [healthy.walla.co](https://healthy.walla.co.il/item/3656679)
- The same survey finds that 66% of donors after October 7 are aged 18–29 (versus a pre‑war landscape where 88% of donors were over 50), and around 22% of donors report increasing their donation amounts, pointing to both generational change and heightened perceived need. [healthy.walla.co](https://healthy.walla.co.il/item/3656679)

### Funding pressures and field imbalances

- The Walla survey indicates that while total giving has increased for war‑related causes, some long‑term welfare and health NGOs (e.g. cancer and disability organizations) are experiencing a relative decline in donations, suggesting cause substitution rather than pure net growth. [healthy.walla.co](https://healthy.walla.co.il/item/3656679)
- JGive’s 2025 annual data shows around 400 million NIS donated via its platform in 2025, with about 125 million NIS (31%) coming from foreign donors, and a sharp 240% increase in donations made in the form of shares (85 million NIS), reflecting sophisticated tax‑optimized giving among high‑income donors. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/activism/article/r1ke7tr11mg)

- Within JGive’s “Platinum” donor tier, 667 major donors contributed roughly 302 million NIS in 2025, and contributions to donor‑advised funds (קרנות נתינה פרטיות) rose by about 40% compared to the previous year, signaling consolidation of giving in the hands of a relatively small, affluent segment. [globes.co](https://www.globes.co.il/news/article.aspx?did=1001543805)
- JFN Israel’s war‑related mapping reports (e.g. on mental health and resilience grants) document a strong concentration of philanthropic flows into emergency response, resilience and mental health, and call out the risk that long‑term social infrastructure and advocacy may be underfunded once the initial emergency phase passes. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/%D7%97%D7%95%D7%A1%D7%9F-%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%AA.pdf)

### Government support and state–civil society relations

- JDC’s “מיזם ממשלה חברה אזרחית” (Government–Civil Society initiative) outlines attempts since the mid‑2010s to structure more coherent partnerships, multi‑year contracts and joint planning processes between ministries and NGOs, especially in welfare and employment services. [thejoint.org](https://www.thejoint.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%9E%D7%9A-%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%9D-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%96%D7%9D-%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%9C%D7%94-%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99%D7%AA1-compressed-1.pdf)
- JFN’s “Shrinking Spaces for Civil Society in Israel” (2023) highlights legislative and political pressures that can constrain foreign funding and advocacy NGO activity, noting that many civil society organizations rely heavily on foreign donations from individuals, corporations, foundations and states. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/Democracy-in-Crisis-Shrinking-spaces-for-civil-society-in-Israel-2023.pdf)

- War‑time coordination documents on civil society activity in חרבות ברזל (e.g. the council for volunteering’s “דו״ח תמונת מצב חברה אזרחית”) emphasize both the strength and fragility of the NGO ecosystem: rapid mobilization, but reliance on ad‑hoc funding, volunteers and patchy government support. [inri.orc.org](https://inri.orc.org.il/media/x32btpg1/%D7%93%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%AA%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%A6%D7%91-%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99%D7%AA-%D7%AA%D7%93%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9A.pdf)
- These analyses collectively suggest that while the state increasingly contracts NGOs for service delivery, advocacy and rights‑based organizations face a more contested environment and more dependence on foreign philanthropy. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/Democracy-in-Crisis-Shrinking-spaces-for-civil-society-in-Israel-2023.pdf)

### Staffing and capacity

- Civil society war‑activity reports and JDC/ELKA commentary refer to NGOs operating under staffing shortages, burnout and high turnover, particularly in front‑line welfare, health and resilience organizations whose workload rose sharply after October 7. [ivolunteer.org](https://ivolunteer.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/%D7%A4%D7%A2%D7%99%D7%9C%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%94%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%9C-%D7%91%D7%9E%D7%9C%D7%97%D7%9E%D7%AA-%D7%97%D7%A8%D7%91%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%96%D7%9C_2023-002.pdf)
- At the same time, the data portal and yearbook underline that many NGOs began from volunteer bases and remain structurally under‑resourced in professional back‑office functions (finance, data, digital), leading to chronic capacity constraints outside the top tier of organizations. [ffi.org](https://ffi.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%9F-%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-2018.pdf)

### Digital transformation and data use

- JGive’s growth—from 102 million NIS in a single month (October 2023) to 400 million NIS across 2025 and 280,000 platform users—illustrates rapid adoption of online fundraising tools among both NGOs and donors. [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/activism/article/r1ke7tr11mg)
- The sharp increase in share‑based giving and donor‑advised funds via digital platforms points to a tech‑savvy donor segment that expects streamlined, data‑rich, and tax‑optimized digital experiences. [globes.co](https://www.globes.co.il/news/article.aspx?did=1001543805)

- JDC‑ELKA’s decision to open weekly‑updated raw datasets on all NGOs (including financials and foreign‑government donations) reflects a policy push toward data transparency and evidence‑based philanthropy and governance. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)
- JPPI’s and JFN’s recent reports make extensive use of such datasets and platform data, indicating a growing ecosystem of analytical intermediaries that model giving flows and NGO capacities, even if most small NGOs themselves lack in‑house data skills. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Philantropic-Giving-for-Resiliance-Mental-Health-JFN-IL-Mapping-HE.pdf)

**Product angle:** there is an emerging demand for tools that help small‑ and mid‑size NGOs with: fundraising analytics, donor segmentation, impact reporting, compliance (ניהול תקין, foreign‑donation reporting), and basic digital infrastructure—areas where current sophistication is concentrated in a minority of large organizations. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Philantropic-Giving-for-Resiliance-Mental-Health-JFN-IL-Mapping-HE.pdf)

## Key intermediaries and support organizations

### Data and infrastructure intermediaries

- Guidestar Israel (operated by משרד המשפטים) is the central public portal for NGO information, built on רשם העמותות / רשם ההקדשות data and enriched by voluntary disclosures from NGOs themselves; it is the backbone for virtually all later analyses and yearbooks. [guidestar.org](https://www.guidestar.org.il/search-malkars)
- JDC‑ELKA’s “מאגר מידע – עמותות וחברות לתועלת הציבור” provides structured, research‑grade datasets derived from those registries, updated weekly and explicitly intended to support researchers, policymakers and sector‑support organizations. [library.socialdirectors](https://library.socialdirectors.org/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%92%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2-%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%95%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%A6%D7%99%D7%91%D7%95%D7%A8/)

- JGive is a leading donation platform connecting donors and NGOs; its annual reports, as cited in Ynet and Globes, show it functioning as both infrastructure and an analytics hub for giving trends (e.g., war‑time spikes, young donor segments, foreign vs. domestic funding, new instruments like share donations). [ynet.co](https://www.ynet.co.il/activism/article/r1ke7tr11mg)
- Digital philanthropy platforms highlighted in JPPI and JFN reports (including those aggregating thousands of NGOs and hundreds of millions of NIS of donations) act as intermediaries between donors, NGOs and data, but their sophistication is currently concentrated on the donor side more than on the NGO operations side. [jppi.org](https://jppi.org.il/he/%D7%94%D7%A2%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%94-%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%AA-2024/s/6.5/)

### Philanthropy and capacity‑building hubs

- JFN Israel (Jewish Funders Network Israel) produces mapping reports—for example, on grants in mental health and resilience—which diagnose fragmentation, short‑termism, and lack of coordination across funders, and explicitly call for better shared data, multi‑year commitments and support for NGO capacity beyond emergency response. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/%D7%97%D7%95%D7%A1%D7%9F-%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%AA.pdf)
- The Joint (ג׳וינט ישראל, including אלכ״א) collaborates with Guidestar and academic partners on sector‑wide surveys like the 2018 yearbook and promotes programs that strengthen NGO management, financial planning and partnership with government. [thejoint.org](https://www.thejoint.org.il/news/17/)

- Sector‑wide civil society snapshots led by bodies like the המועצה הישראלית להתנדבות and governmental partners (e.g., משרד הרווחה, רשות החירום הלאומית) identify volunteer management, coordination in crises and insufficient preparedness of NGOs as recurring challenges. [inri.orc.org](https://inri.orc.org.il/media/x32btpg1/%D7%93%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%AA%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%A6%D7%91-%D7%97%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%94-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99%D7%AA-%D7%AA%D7%93%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9A.pdf)
- Analyses of “shrinking space” for civil society by JFN and others note dependence on foreign funding, regulatory uncertainty and political polarization as structural vulnerabilities, particularly for rights‑based and advocacy organizations. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/Democracy-in-Crisis-Shrinking-spaces-for-civil-society-in-Israel-2023.pdf)

### What gaps do they highlight for small–mid NGOs?

Across these intermediaries and reports, several recurring tooling/support gaps emerge:

- **Financial and data literacy:** many NGOs lack the capacity to interpret their own financials, manage compliance (ניהול תקין, foreign‑donation reporting) and produce impact reports that satisfy institutional funders—JDC‑ELKA’s decision to open raw datasets is partly a response to this, but the tools to consume them are still thin at the NGO level. [jfn.org](https://jfn.org.il/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Philantropic-Giving-for-Resiliance-Mental-Health-JFN-IL-Mapping-HE.pdf)
- **Digital fundraising and CRM:** while platforms like JGive provide donor‑side tools, smaller NGOs often lack integrated CRMs, donor‑journey analytics, and content infrastructure to convert digital traffic into sustained support, a gap implicitly visible in the skewed distribution of sophisticated instruments (like share donations and donor‑advised funds) toward wealthy, digitally fluent donors and larger NGOs. [globes.co](https://www.globes.co.il/news/article.aspx?did=1001543805)

- **Strategic, multi‑year funding and resilience:** JFN’s mapping work repeatedly warns that emergency giving surges (e.g., post‑October 7) but long‑term psychosocial support, advocacy and community‑building organizations face unstable, short‑term funding and lack tools to model and communicate their longer‑term impact.
